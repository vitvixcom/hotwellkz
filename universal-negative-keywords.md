# Universal Negative Keyword List · Service Businesses


A consolidated, copy-paste-ready negative keyword list for any service business running
Google Ads (plumber, HVAC, electrician, roofer, lawyer, dentist, cleaner, contractor, etc.).


**Sourced from 15 PPC blog posts and agency lists (see [Sources](#sources) at bottom).**


**Use at the account or campaign level. Match type = BROAD unless otherwise noted.**


---


## How to use this list


1. **Day 1**: paste the **150 universal terms** below into a Shared Negative Keyword List in Google Ads (Tools → Shared Library → Negative keyword lists)
2. **Attach the list to your campaign** (one click — see Step 4 of SOP §A)
3. **Run for 1-2 weeks** with these defaults
4. **Pull Search Terms report weekly**: add new junk you see, prune any genuine money keywords this list accidentally blocks
5. **Most accounts stabilize at ~150-250 negatives** after 60 days


> **Quality > quantity.** One broad-match `jobs` blocks thousands of irrelevant queries.
> Adding 50 exact-match versions of niche-specific junk is busywork.

## C. Geographic negatives — fill in YOUR list


If you serve **Toronto + 50km**, block all the cities/regions OUTSIDE that area
that compete for your service keywords.


Template:
```
[major city you don't serve]
[major city you don't serve]
[major city you don't serve]
[neighboring metro you don't serve]
[state/province you don't serve]
[country code you don't serve]
```

```

montreal
ottawa
winnipeg
hamilton
london ontario
kitchener
windsor
USA
united states
new york
california
texas

```


Google's location targeting (presence-only, 50km) catches most of this anyway —
but `presence` doesn't block a Toronto resident **searching for a Calgary plumber**.
Geo negatives close that loophole.

## D. Competitor brand negatives — case-by-case


You probably **don't** want to add competitor brand names as negatives in your
primary SKAG campaign — those searches are mid-funnel and convert at 2-3× higher
than generic queries.


You **do** want to add them as **negatives on every other campaign except your dedicated
"Competitor Conquest" campaign**, so the queries route to the right ads.


If you don't run a Conquest campaign yet, add the top 5-10 local competitors as broad
negatives at the account level to avoid wasting brand-mismatched clicks:


```
[competitor 1]
[competitor 2]
[competitor 3]
[competitor 4]
[competitor 5]
```

## E. Match types · what to use when


| Type | Use for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Broad** (default) | Single-word universal junk | `jobs`, `free`, `how`, `diy` |
| **Phrase** (`"in quotes"`) | Multi-word phrases where word order matters | `"how to fix"`, `"plumber salary"` |
| **Exact** (`[brackets]`) | Precise irrelevant queries you want to block but not their variants | `[plumber jobs near me]` |


> **Watch out**: Negative broad match does NOT include close variants or synonyms.
> If you broad-negative `jobs`, the queries `job` and `hiring` still trigger your ad unless
> they're also negated. That's why a comprehensive list still has both `job` and `jobs`.

## F. Things NOT to add (common mistakes)


These look like they should be negatives but they actually block legitimate buyer queries:


| Term | Why you might want to add it | Why you shouldn't |
|---|---|---|
| `affordable` | "cheap shoppers" | "Affordable plumber" = budget-conscious but ready to buy |
| `near me` | seems too vague | It's literally a top-3 highest-converting modifier |
| `quote` | "researching" | "Get a quote" = lead form intent |
| `price` / `pricing` | "tire-kickers" | "Plumber pricing Toronto" = late-funnel, high intent |
| `cost` | same | same — high-intent comparison shopping |
| `best` | "researching" | "Best plumber Toronto" = comparison shopper close to deciding |
| `hire` | "researching how to hire" | "Hire a plumber" = direct buyer intent |
| `local` | seems too vague | High geographic intent |
| `emergency` | obvious | This is your **best** keyword, not a negative |

## G. Maintaining the list (weekly · 15 min)


1. **Open Google Ads** → Insights & reports → Search terms
2. **Date range**: last 7 days
3. **Sort by Cost descending**
4. For each term:
  - **Did it convert?** ✓ Skip
  - **Did it spend $20+ with 0 conversion?** ✗ Add as a campaign negative (phrase match for multi-word, broad for single)
  - **Is it borderline (low cost, no conv, but plausible intent)?** Mark and wait one more week
5. **Bulk-add** the negatives in one operation
6. **Done**


Most accounts find 5-15 new negatives per week for the first 6 weeks, then it tapers to 1-3.
